Nauru Camps and Their Cruelty.

What is the Nauru Refugee facility?

The Nauru Refugee Facility is one of many facilities built to house asylum seekers or refugees coming from different countries from all over the world. These asylum seekers could be coming to Australia for many different reasons, these reasons can include:

  • Fleeing from conflict

  • Seeking better living standards

  • Escaping poverty

  • To flee from a corrupt government

  • To seek better lives for their families

Whatever the reason the refugees deserve to be allowed into Australia and given a good life as an Australian citizen, having the same benefits as a normal citizen should have.

The issues with the Nauru camps

Nauru camp is a major problem for its inhabitants, people have been there for more than 5 years starting in 2014 and only started being allowed to leave the facility in 2015. Even though they are allowed to leave the facility the island is incredibly small and has very little infrastructure. As you can imagine, being stuck on a island in what seems to be a prison for criminals can be stressful and it can takes its toll on your mental health. Research and investigations into the facility has shown mental health issues such as suicidal thoughts and depression in children as young as 8 - 10 years of age.
